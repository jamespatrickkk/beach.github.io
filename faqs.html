FAQs About Planning Your Trip to El Nido

The best time to visit El Nido is during the dry season, from November to May, when the weather is sunny and the seas are calm.

You can take a direct flight from Manila to El Nido Airport or fly to Puerto Princesa and take a 5–6 hour van/bus ride to El Nido.

Popular tours include Tour A, B, C, and D. Each tour takes you to beautiful islands, lagoons, and beaches around Bacuit Bay.

Budget depends on accommodation and tours. On average, a 3-day trip can cost around PHP 10,000–15,000 per person for budget travelers.

Pack light clothing, swimwear, sunscreen, a hat, water shoes, and waterproof bags. Don’t forget a camera for all the beautiful scenery.

Yes, El Nido town proper has a variety of restaurants, cafes, and small shops. Island tours often include packed lunches or stops at small local eateries.

Tap water in El Nido is **not recommended for drinking**. It’s best to drink bottled or filtered water to avoid stomach issues. Many hotels and restaurants provide safe drinking water.
